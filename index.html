What makes a good design?

Every website has a different purpose, but they all want to get and keep your attention. Some aspects of good design are universal, such as readability and clarity. Other aspects change with the times: for example, in the 1990's I preferred Yahoo's busy landing page to Google's sparse one, because I was familiar with physical books where denser content meant more comprehensive content - and I had not yet intuited a notion of using links to spread content across pages.

Principles of good design

  • grid_on Whitespace and layout

    Lay out your content so that there's a lot of whitespace. Don't write long sentences.
    Consider bullet-pointing, using parallelism to ensure readability.
    If you're not using Materialize, then use CSS commands like "margin" and "padding."
    Be thoughtful about centering text.

  • format_size Font and text

    Use consistent font styles and sizes.
    Choose fonts that are readable and professional.
    Use font size and formatting to emphasize the content that matters most.

  • format_paint Color

    Choose a consistent color theme.
    Make your colors enhance your content, but content comes first.
    Think about where the eye is drawn on your page, and decide whether it's where you want the eye to be drawn.

  • image Images

    Choose images that support your purpose, if you choose to use images at all.
    Use auto sizing of your images to ensure they fit cleanly on the page on all different screens.
    Consider putting text on top of images when the images serve as background.
    Make it clear when an image is a link.

  • gesture Decorative elements

    Don't add decorative elements unless they serve your content.
    Start off simple with break lines or cards.
    Use all decorative elements consistently, so that each element serves a specific purpose.
